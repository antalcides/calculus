The algebra associated with secant lines (and average velocities) can sometimes be simplified if we designate the variable \(h\) to be the run between the two points (or the length of the time interval). With this designation we have \(x_1-x_0=h\) which gives us \(x_1=x_0+h\). Making these substitutions we get (1.3.1). The expression on the right side of (1.3.1) is called the difference quotient for \(f\).\begin{equation}\frac{\fe{f}{x_1}-\fe{f}{x_0}}{x_1-x_0}=\frac{\fe{f}{x_0+h}-\fe{f}{x_0}}{h}\tag{1.3.1}\end{equation}
