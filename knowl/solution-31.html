Solution2.14.1.15.1

\begin{align*} &\phantom{={}}\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{3x^3+2x}{3x-2x^3}&&\text{Can't apply LL A1--A6; form is $\tfrac{\infty}{\infty}$}\\ &=\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{3x^3+2x}{3x-2x^3}\\ &=\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{3x^3+2x}{3x-2x^3}\frac{\sfrac{1}{x^3}}{\sfrac{1}{x^3}}\\ &=\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{3+\frac{2}{x^2}}{\frac{3}{x^2}-2}\\ &=\frac{\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\left(3+\frac{2}{x^2}\right)}{\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\left(\frac{3}{x^2}-2\right)}&&\text{LL A5}\\ &=\frac{\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}3+\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{2}{x^2}}{\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}\frac{3}{x^2}-\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty}2}&&\text{LL A1 and A2}\\ &=\frac{3+0}{0-2}&&\text{LL R2 and R3}\\ &=-\frac{3}{2}&&\text{This limit exists.} \end{align*}
